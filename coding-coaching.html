Your Journey




Why Us Over a Bootcamp



Weekly 1-on-1 calls with A mentor

With bootcamps you get minimal face-time with the instructors.


With us, you get this every week.

With you for a YEAR

Bootcamps leave you high and dry when it's time to find a job.


You'll have us throughout the job search process, and then some. We're confident you'll get a job within a year, even learning part-time (10+ hours a week)

Self-paced

Bootcamps operate on a set curriculum and a quick pace, so you will likely fall behind.


With us, you never move onto the next topic until you're ready.

Never get stuck

It's hard for bootcamp instructors to devote a lot of time to help you debug things.


With us, we never want to let you get stuck. You can do ad hoc calls whenever needed.

Professional code reviews

Bootcamps don't review all the code for your projects, so you're unaware of better ways you could have written your code.


We review all your project code and provide feedback so you become a real professional programmer.

No entry requirements

Bootcamps have rigorous entry requirements.


All we have is a personality interview to make sure we like you :)

Our curriculum is designed to take even complete newbies from zero to hero. We prefer to teach everything in the exact order we think is best, rather than making you inefficiently learn a bunch of information before getting in.

Accountability and emotional support

You will be in constant communication with a mentor to make sure you're always supported.




Your Instructor



Jared has always put deep thought into the many life paths that one can take. His own journey of self-discovery can parallel almost anyone’s experience of struggling to get where they want to go.


Always having an affinity for scientific topics, Jared decided to study mechanical engineering at McGill University in Montreal, Canada. Not excited by the mechanical engineer’s career path, Jared decided to embark on a year and a half of world travel and self-discovery.


At the culmination of his trip, he realized that learning to code would provide him all the opportunity he was looking for.


Jared spent 3 months learning on his own, followed by a 13-week bootcamp in NYC to accelerate his learning. He then spent 4 stressful months looking for a job, finally securing a 6-figure-paying position at a healthcare company in Seattle.


He worked hard in the industry for a number of years and had the good fortune to work closely with accomplished ex-employees of big tech companies and founders of successful startups.


When he was ready to be an entrepreneur, Jared left the workforce and launched a coding program designed to help people along the same path he took, but without all the stress.


He believes it is essential for people to be supported through the entire journey of learning coding until they've landed happily into the industry.
